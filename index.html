Skeletons and the Chalice

In 2024, archaeologists discovered an intact tomb beneath the Al-Khazneh monument in Petra, Jordan. Inside, they found 12 human skeletons and a ceremonial chalice over 2,000 years old. The arrangement of the bodies and the presence of the chalice suggest a collective funerary ritual belonging to the ancient Nabataean civilization. Some have described the object as resembling the mythical “Holy Grail,” although there is no historical connection to that tradition. This discovery is crucial for understanding the funerary and spiritual practices of the Nabataeans.
